Design Principles

By Jewel Victorino

White Space and Clean Design


A clean website should incorporate blank spaces know as "white space" between content. The breaks give a users mind and eye a break between visual stimulating imagery and letters. White space provides balance and can help highlight items of importance for the user. When white space is utilitzed in designs, the simplicity of the site offers more opporunities to showcase more photography or graphics.

Clark Realty Website

One website that I have seen incorporate the use of white space is a local real estate company in my area. The top of the website focuses on the information which requires user input in order to utilize the website in searching for properties for sale. The utilization of a white background helps to focus the eye on filling out the form. Two similar colored buttons with the words "Search" stands out as the focus of the page.



White space is also incorporated in between photo blocks featuring different types of properties. The photos themselves already have vivid colors and therefore stand out on their own. There is also very little colors that are incoporated in the overall design. The photos of homes and the landscapes offer the visual appeal to the user. This helps to focus on what is being sold in the website.

PARC: Repetition


Repetition in website creation helps to promote a theme and provides co-hesiveness to the design. Repeating colors, fonts, or similar groupings of photos can promote branding in a variety of ways.

Adidas Website

The sports brand Adidas incorporates creative ways in promoting their logo and brand throughout the website. The colors of black and white are repeated in the logo, font, and background. The blocks containing merchadise a subtle greyish-white so showcase their black & white motif.



The consistency in repeating their logo colors also extends to the clothing modeled and merchadise displayed in blocks are repeated at the bottom of the website. All the items featured for sale are in the black or white color although they come in other colors as well. There are a few pops of color to break up parts of the site but for the most part, everything featured is repeated with the black and white theme.

PARC: Contrast


Contrast is being able to utilize colors for the background and foreground in a way differentiates the two. Typically, the areas of importance should be easily seen by using either color, various font sizes or how or where images are placed in the design. Contrast gives the viewer the focal point of a design and to call their attention to a section or in the case of a website a call to action on a particular part of the site.

Bath & Body Works Website

The Bath & Body Works website exemplifies contrast. There are 2 bold colors in direct contrast with each other. The page immediately draws the eye to stop and really take a look at what the page is offering which are the sales items. The contrast colors of the pink and yellow background coupled with the black and white contrast of the font helps to balance the contrast of the page. Also there are contrasts in the shapes of the rectangle versus the circle in the upper left side corner. Contrast also continues in the visual heirarchy of font sizes.



At the bottom of the website, the contrast continues but in a different variation. Rather than bright contrasting background colors, the background color is a solid white. The featured instgram posts feature the brigh colors of products and continue the contrast through the photography rather than the background colors.
